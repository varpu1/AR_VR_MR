Here are some papers and websites that you can read to futher your knowledge on why augmeted reality, virtual reality, and mixed reality should be used in educational institutions

  • Allen, S. (2004), Designs for learning: Studying science museum exhibits that do more than entertain. Sci. Ed., 88: S17-S33. doi:10.1002/sce.20016 . Describes the thinking behind the scenes of a museum interactive exhibit.
  • Brenneman, Ross. “Gallup Student Poll Finds Engagement in School Dropping by Grade Level.” Education Week, Editorial Project in Education, 20 June 2018, www.edweek.org/ew/articles/2016/03/23/gallup-student-poll-finds-engagement-in-school.html. Shows findings of a survey on the engagement levels of students.
  • Brown, Liza. “Difference between AR, VR, and MR.” Filmora , Filmora Wondershare, 24 Nov. 2017, filmora.wondershare.com/virtual-reality/difference-between-vr-ar-mr.html A picture to help visualize difference between the different realities
  • Wu, Hsin-Kai, et al. "Current Status, Opportunities and Challenges of Augmented Reality in Education." Computers & Education, vol. 62, 2013, pp. 41-49. ProQuest, http://search.proquest.com.ezproxy.rit.edu/docview/1413415101?accountid=108 doi:http://dx.doi.org/10.1016/j.compedu.2012.10.024 . Shows that AR offers affordances of presence, immediacy, and immersion.
  • University of Wisconsin - Milwaukee. "Tracking The Reasons Many Girls Avoid Science And Math." ScienceDaily. ScienceDaily, 8 September 2008. . Gave me the perspective of girls towards STEM subjects. Gave incite into how to combat girls aversion to the STEM subjects
  • Tosco, Justin E.. (2015). The Effects of Technology on Engagement and Retention Among Upper Elementary Montessori Students.. Retrieved from Sophia, the St. Catherine University repository website: https://sophia.stkate.edu/maed/122 Corrolation between engagement and technology.
  • She, Hsiao-Ching, and Yi-Zen Chen. "The Impact of Multimedia Effect on Science Learning: Evidence from Eye Movements." Computers & Education, vol. 53, no. 4, 2009, pp. 1297-1307. ProQuest, http://search.proquest.com.ezproxy.rit.edu/docview/61865614?accountid=108 doi:http://dx.doi.org/10.1016/j.compedu.2009.06.012 How interactive and immersive leaning has an effect on the student.
  • Ryan. “The State of STEM Education Told Through 12 Stats.” ID Tech, 6 Dec. 2017, www.idtech.com/blog/stem-education-statistics . STEM in correlation to girls, I used it to give a statistic of women in computer science
  • Holstermann, Nina, Dietmar Grube, and Susanne Bogeholz. "Hands-on Activities and their Influence on Students' Interest." Research in Science Education, vol. 40, no. 5, 2010, pp. 743-757. ProQuest, http://search.proquest.com.ezproxy.rit.edu/docview/851230925?accountid=108 doi:http://dx.doi.org/10.1007/s11165-009-9142-0 . To show student intrest in corralation to hands on interaction.
